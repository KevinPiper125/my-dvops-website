Live Stream Presence

Built a live broadcasting presence on Twitch and YouTube from the ground up.

← Back to Home

Overview

The Project

This wasn't just hitting "Go Live" and seeing what happens. Building a real streaming presence on Twitch and YouTube meant learning an entirely new skill set from scratch — audio engineering, video production, streaming software configuration, and graphic design for overlays and channel branding.

Starting with zero production experience, I worked through the technical side of live broadcasting: audio mixing, scene composition, stream quality settings, and creating the visual assets that make a channel feel professional and consistent.

What I Built

Skills Developed

Audio/Video Production: Learning microphone placement, gain staging, noise reduction, and how to balance audio sources in a live context. On the video side — scene transitions, camera settings, and encoding for both Twitch and YouTube's different quality requirements.

Graphic Design: Designing stream overlays, alerts, channel banners, profile graphics, and thumbnails. Starting in tools I'd never used before and building up a consistent visual identity that I could own and iterate on.

Streaming Software: Configuring OBS and learning how scenes, sources, and filters work together to produce a clean broadcast. Troubleshooting dropped frames, audio sync issues, and bitrate settings all became part of the process.

Why It Matters

The Bigger Picture

Content creation is how I document and share my journey in tech. It keeps me accountable, forces me to understand things well enough to talk about them, and connects me with a community of people on a similar path.

The skills are also genuinely technical — anyone who thinks streaming is just pointing a camera at themselves hasn't tried to debug an audio routing issue at 11pm before going live.
